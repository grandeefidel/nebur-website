Our Story

We're Redefining
Logistics in Nigeria

We started Nebur because we kept experiencing — and hearing about — the same frustration: parcels lost, riders who don't show up, no updates, no accountability. We knew Nigeria deserved better.

How It Started

Built out of frustration. Driven by purpose.


Nebur was born in 2023 from a simple, shared frustration. Our founders — both e-commerce sellers and frequent parcel senders — found themselves constantly battling unreliable couriers, lost packages, and zero customer service. Every time a delivery went wrong, the answer was a shrug.

So they decided to fix it themselves. Starting with a small team of riders in Lagos and a very simple promise — show up on time, communicate clearly, and make it right if something goes wrong — Nebur made its first 100 deliveries in January 2023.

Word spread fast. Sellers on Instagram and Jiji started switching. Small businesses in Lekki and Victoria Island signed up. By the end of 2023, Nebur had completed over 10,000 deliveries and opened its first dedicated operations hub in Lagos Island. The Abuja hub followed in mid-2024, and our express same-day service launched in early 2025.

Today, Nebur is building the logistics infrastructure Nigeria's digital economy has always needed — reliable, transparent, and built to scale across the entire continent.

"Nigeria's digital economy is growing faster than its logistics infrastructure. We built Nebur to close that gap — one delivered parcel at a time."
— Ruben Adeleke, Co-founder & CEO

Our Journey

From idea to nationwide operations

Every milestone was hard-won. Here's how Nebur grew from a Lagos experiment into a national logistics brand.

2023

Company Founded

Ruben Adeleke and Bisi Okonkwo launch Nebur from a Lagos apartment with six riders, one van, and a very big idea about what Nigerian logistics could be.

2023 — Q4

First 10,000 Deliveries & Lagos Hub Opens

Nebur hits 10,000 successful deliveries and opens its first physical operations hub on Lagos Island, serving the entire Lagos metropolitan area.

2024 — Mid

Abuja Hub Launched

Expansion north — the FCT hub opens, enabling standard and express deliveries across Abuja, Gwagwalada, and surrounding areas. B2B contracts start flowing.

2025

Express Same-Day Service & E-commerce Fulfilment

Nebur launches same-day delivery in all three major hubs and introduces full e-commerce warehousing and fulfilment services for online sellers. 50,000+ deliveries completed.

2026 — Planned

Pan-African Expansion

Planning underway to extend Nebur's network to Ghana, Kenya, and South Africa — bringing the same standard of reliable last-mile logistics to more African markets.
